Role details

Open Hours Sessions

Teaching the Terminal

  • Led open hours sessions: function by function, from navigation to advanced data pulls.
  • Covered historical data retrieval across equities and fixed income: price and volume histories, dividends, and earnings timelines.
  • Taught economic estimates workflows: GDP, CPI, PMIs, and analyst revision trends for macro positioning.
  • Built student BQL proficiency: custom queries, equity screens, and bulk data exports.
Model Building

Stock Probability Model

  • Watching high-volatility equity behavior on the Terminal led directly to building a stock probability model independently.
  • The model quantifies expected move distributions across regimes, incorporating momentum signals, volatility clustering, and information asymmetry.
  • Built from first principles. No external API. The thesis came from patterns I watched live.
Quant Research Pathway

From open hours to research lab

  • The depth from this role is what got me the quant research internship at UML, turning terminal fluency into a research credential.
  • In the internship: VIX term structure, implied vs. realized vol spreads, and vol behavior during risk-off events.
  • Developed theories on extracting arbitrage profit from vol mispricing: dislocations between implied surfaces and realized distributions.
What This Role Built

Terminal fluency as a research foundation

Most people use Bloomberg as a lookup tool. Teaching it forced something different: you have to know the why behind every function because students ask the hard questions. That's what turned terminal operator into model builder into quant researcher. Not a leap. A progression.
