CCR details

The Founding

UML's first capital markets org

  • Founded Commonwealth Capital Research as UMass Lowell's first student organization dedicated exclusively to capital markets education and recruiting.
  • Built the organization's structure, membership pipeline, curriculum, and external partnerships from the ground up in the first semester.
Institutional Recruiting

Franklin Templeton & MFS Investments

  • Secured institutional recruiting pipelines with Franklin Templeton and MFS Investments — connecting UML students directly to two of the largest asset managers in Boston.
  • These pipelines didn't exist at UML before CCR. Building them required direct outreach, relationship development, and a credible organizational pitch.
Competition

St. John's PE Case Competition, NYC

  • Led the CCR team to the St. John's Private Equity Case Competition in New York City.
  • Built a full LBO model and pro-forma financial statements for a corporate carveout of two inspection & certification companies.
  • Competed against teams from target schools — delivering a live deal presentation under competition conditions.
What it takes

Starting something that didn't exist

There's a specific kind of difficulty to founding something institutional at a school that doesn't have it yet — you're not joining a club, you're building the infrastructure that didn't exist. CCR is the proof that UML has the talent for this work. The recruiting pipelines are there. The competition record is there. The only thing missing before was the organization to make it visible.
